Angle Sum = 360°

Drag any vertex to reshape the quadrilateral. The four angles always add to exactly 360° — no matter what shape you make.

Try every shape: rectangle, kite, trapezium, a wild irregular shape — the angles always sum to exactly 360°. Show the diagonal to see why: it splits the quadrilateral into two triangles, each summing to 180°.

∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360° always, for any quadrilateral
Why is the sum always 360°? Draw diagonal AC — it splits the quadrilateral into two triangles. △ABC: ∠A₁ + ∠B + ∠C₁ = 180° △ACD: ∠A₂ + ∠C₂ + ∠D = 180° Adding both: (∠A₁+∠A₂) + ∠B + (∠C₁+∠C₂) + ∠D = 360° That is: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360° — for any quadrilateral, always.
A nice consequence: If three angles of a quadrilateral are 90° each, their sum is 270°. The fourth angle must be 360° − 270° = 90°. So a quadrilateral with three right angles must have four right angles — it is a rectangle.
