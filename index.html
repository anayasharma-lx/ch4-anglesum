Why is the sum always 360°? Draw diagonal AC. It splits the quadrilateral into two triangles: △ABC and △ACD. △ABC: ∠A₁ + ∠B + ∠C₁ = 180° △ACD: ∠A₂ + ∠C₂ + ∠D = 180° Add: (∠A₁+∠A₂) + ∠B + (∠C₁+∠C₂) + ∠D = 360° That is: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C + ∠D = 360°, for any quadrilateral.
Drag any vertex to reshape the quadrilateral
Why can't a quadrilateral have three right angles and one non-right angle? If three angles are 90°, their sum is 270°. The fourth must be 360° − 270° = 90° as well. So the fourth angle is forced to be 90° too — making it a rectangle.
